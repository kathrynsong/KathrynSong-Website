Kathryn Song

Pre-Medicine

Hello! My name is Kathryn Song and I am currently a second year pre-medical student at the University of California, Riverside. I am majoring in Biological Sciences and I have many experiences pertaining to my major and my field of interest. I am currently involved in the Women in STEM organization, in which I am a mentor guiding global partnerships with schools to empower young women and increase female representation in STEM. Here, I am collaborating with 77 WiSTEM chapters worldwide to implement activism proposals, advancing gender equality, along with organizing events and meetings highlighting our three main initiatives: outreach, mentorship, and networking.

In addition to my Women in STEM organization, I have experience as a medical assistant at Valley OBGYN Medical Group. As a medical assistant, I aid in clinical tasks including taking patient histories, measuring vital signs, and administering medications. I also manage patient registration and insurance verification processes and maintain medical inventory and supplies by monitoring stock levels, ordering supplies, and tracking usage, ensuring a seamless healthcare experience. In addition to this role, I am currently a Laboratory Research Assistant for the US Department of Agriculture. Here, I am utilizing advanced RNA sequencing to analyze guar transcriptomes, enhancing knowledge of plant salt tolerance. I am leading genetic research that identifies genes for cultivating salt-tolerant guar in diverse soil salinity environments by performing tasks such as the preparation of biological samples for laboratory analysis in Dr. Devinder Sandhu's agricultural research project.

My prior experiences include my Dynamic Genome laboratory research project, as I located the mPing of a specific rice DNA, which was crossed between rice strains of Nipponbare and HEG4. My role was to validate results through PCR tests and gel electrophoresis, while gaining experience in pipetting and dilutions. Along with this, I have clinical dermatology shadow experience that I did over the past summer. Here, I engaged in discussions on educational readings and patient cases, enhancing critical thinking in clinical care. I also observed the physician diagnose skin conditions, deepening knowledge of dermatology and improving patient care and facilitated patient flow by scheduling appointments and ensuring timely medical examinations and procedures. Along with all of my experiences, I am BLS Certified by the American Heart Association and I am Dermatology Assistant Course Certified. I am also fluent in English and intermediate proficiency in Spanish and Korean, which helps in my communication with patients at clinics. Lastly, I have proficiency of laboratory techniques through my previous laboratory projects, such as pipetting, genetic information transfer, DNA polymorphisms. I hope to find a career in medicine and gain more experience throughout my journey to become a doctor.
